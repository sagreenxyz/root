## Overview

The **nursing process** is a systematic, evidence-based framework that guides nurses through clinical decision-making. Its five phases are commonly remembered by the acronym **ADPIE**:

1. **Assessment** – Collect subjective and objective data from the patient.
2. **Diagnosis** – Analyze data to identify actual or potential health problems (NANDA-I nursing diagnoses).
3. **Planning** – Set measurable, patient-centered goals and select nursing interventions.
4. **Implementation** – Carry out the nursing care plan and document actions taken.
5. **Evaluation** – Determine whether goals were met; revise the plan as needed.

---

## Key Concepts

### Assessment
- **Subjective data**: What the patient reports (symptoms, feelings, concerns).
- **Objective data**: Measurable findings (vital signs, lab values, physical exam).
- Sources include the patient, family, health records, and the care team.

### Nursing Diagnosis
- Uses **NANDA-I** taxonomy for standardized language.
- Three-part format: *Problem + Etiology + Signs/Symptoms* (PES statement).
- Distinguishes **actual**, **risk**, and **health-promotion** diagnoses.

### Planning
- Goals must be **SMART** (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic, Time-bound).
- Distinguish **short-term** goals (hours to days) from **long-term** goals (weeks to discharge).
- Nursing interventions selected from evidence-based references (NIC taxonomy).

### Implementation
- Includes **independent**, **dependent**, and **collaborative** nursing actions.
- All interventions must be documented in the clinical record (legal and ethical requirement).
- Ongoing reassessment during implementation is essential.

### Evaluation
- Compare patient outcomes to established goals.
- Determine if goals were **met**, **partially met**, or **not met**.
- Revise the care plan based on evaluation findings — the process is **cyclical**.

---

## Clinical Application Tips

- Always validate assessment findings before formulating a nursing diagnosis.
- Involve the patient and family in goal-setting to improve adherence and outcomes.
- Use the **SBAR** framework (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation) when handing off care.
- Document in real-time whenever possible to ensure accuracy.
